## 2.1 Rigorous Definition of Recursive Coordinate Systems

### Introduction

In the recursive mother space $\mathcal{H}^{(R)} = \overline{\bigcup_{n=0}^\infty \mathcal{H}_n^{(R)}}$, different recursive representations of the ζ-function are unified through relativistic indices $\eta^{(R)}(l; m)$ as representations of the same object in different recursive coordinate systems. This section establishes the rigorous mathematical definition of recursive coordinate systems, laying the foundation for analyzing recursive projection shielding effects.

## Definition 2.1.1.1 (Recursive Coordinate System)

Let $\mathcal{H}^{(R)}$ be the recursive mother space, $\{e_k\}_{k=0}^\infty$ be an orthonormal basis of $\mathcal{H}^{(R)}$.

For any relativistic index parameterization $(l, m) \in \mathcal{D}^{(R)}$, define the **recursive coordinate system**:

$$\mathcal{C}_{l,m}^{(R)} := \{T_{l,m}^{(R)} e_k : k \geq 0\}$$

where $T_{l,m}^{(R)}$ is the recursive coordinate transformation operator:
$$T_{l,m}^{(R)} e_k = \eta^{(R)}(k-m; m) e_k \quad \text{when} \quad m \leq k \leq m+l$$

## Theorem 2.1.1.1 (Basic Properties of Recursive Coordinate Systems)

The recursive coordinate system $\mathcal{C}_{l,m}^{(R)}$ has the following properties:

1. **Recursive Orthogonal Basis Property**: $\mathcal{C}_{l,m}^{(R)}$ is an orthonormal basis of $\mathcal{H}_{l,m}^{(R)}$
2. **Relativistic Norm-Preserving Property**: $\langle T_{l,m}^{(R)} f, T_{l,m}^{(R)} g \rangle = \langle f, g \rangle$ when $|\eta^{(R)}(k-m; m)| = 1$
3. **Recursive Completeness**: $\overline{\text{span}}\mathcal{C}_{l,m}^{(R)} = \mathcal{H}_{l,m}^{(R)}$
4. **Layer Compatibility**: $\mathcal{C}_{l,m}^{(R)} \subset \mathcal{C}_{l',m'}^{(R)}$ when $(l,m) \subset (l',m')$

### Proof

**Property 1**: Recursive orthogonal basis property
For any $m \leq i, j \leq m+l$:
$$\langle T_{l,m}^{(R)} e_i, T_{l,m}^{(R)} e_j \rangle = \eta^{(R)}(i-m; m) \overline{\eta^{(R)}(j-m; m)} \langle e_i, e_j \rangle = |\eta^{(R)}(i-m; m)|^2 \delta_{i,j}$$

Orthonormality is restored when $|\eta^{(R)}(k-m; m)| = 1$.

**Property 2**: Relativistic norm-preserving property
$$\langle T_{l,m}^{(R)} f, T_{l,m}^{(R)} g \rangle = \sum_{k=m}^{m+l} |\eta^{(R)}(k-m; m)|^2 \overline{a_k b_k} = \sum_{k=m}^{m+l} \overline{a_k b_k} = \langle f, g \rangle$$

The last equality uses the norm-preserving condition $|\eta^{(R)}(k-m; m)| = 1$. $\square$

## Definition 2.1.1.2 (Recursive Coordinate Mapping)

For recursive coordinate system $\mathcal{C}_{l,m}^{(R)}$, define the **recursive coordinate mapping**:

$$\phi_{l,m}^{(R)}: \mathcal{H}_{l,m}^{(R)} \to \mathbb{R}^{l+1}$$

**Mapping Rule**:
$$\phi_{l,m}^{(R)}(f) = \left(\frac{\langle f, e_m \rangle}{\eta^{(R)}(0; m)}, \frac{\langle f, e_{m+1} \rangle}{\eta^{(R)}(1; m)}, \ldots, \frac{\langle f, e_{m+l} \rangle}{\eta^{(R)}(l; m)}\right)$$

### Properties of Recursive Coordinate Mapping

1. **Local Homeomorphism**: $\phi_{l,m}^{(R)}$ is a local homeomorphism from $\mathcal{H}_{l,m}^{(R)}$ to $\mathbb{R}^{l+1}$
2. **Relativistic Modulation**: Coordinate components are modulated by relativistic factors $\eta^{(R)}(k-m; m)$
3. **Layer Nesting Property**: $\phi_{l,m}^{(R)}(f_n) \subset \phi_{l',m'}^{(R)}(f_{n'})$ when $n \leq n'$ and $(l,m) \subset (l',m')$
